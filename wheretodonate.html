Where To Donate

Although we provide all the information you could need for your dog, we do not accept donations/are not a charity.

There are numerous dog charities and organizations that you can donate to.

Here are some popular and well-known dog charities where you can consider making a donation:

ASPCA (American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals):

The ASPCA works to rescue and protect animals from abuse, provide veterinary care, promote adoption, and support legislative efforts for animal welfare.

Best Friends Animal Society:

Best Friends Animal Society aims to bring about a time when there are no more homeless pets through initiatives like animal rescue, adoption, spay/neuter programs, and advocacy.

The Morris Animal Foundation:

The Morris Animal Foundation funds scientific research to improve the health and well-being of animals, including dogs. They support studies on various canine health issues and work towards advancing veterinary medicine.

Guide Dogs for the Blind:

This organization provides guide dogs to individuals with visual impairments, enabling them to live more independent lives.

Canine Companions for Independence:

Canine Companions for Independence trains and provides assistance dogs to people with disabilities, including veterans and children with special needs.

Local Animal Shelters and Rescues:

Consider donating to local animal shelters and rescues in your area. These organizations work tirelessly to provide care, find homes for homeless dogs, and promote responsible pet ownership.

Breed-Specific Rescue Organizations:

There are many breed-specific rescue organizations that focus on rescuing and rehoming specific dog breeds. If you have a particular breed in mind, you can search for breed-specific rescue groups that may appreciate your support.

These are just a few examples, and there are countless other dog charities and organizations dedicated to improving the lives of dogs. It's always a good idea to research and choose organizations that align with your values and causes you care about. Additionally, local dog shelters and rescue groups in your area may greatly benefit from your support.
